I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at Université Clermont Auvergne under the supervision of Prof. Éric Gaudron, as part of the ANR project Effective Arithmetic Geometry in Clermont (GAEC).

I obtained my PhD in Mathematics in October 2025 from Università degli studi Roma Tre, under the supervision of Prof. Fabrizio Barroero.

My research focuses on Algebraic Number Theory and Diophantine Geometry, in particular on problems of Unlikely Intersections and Chabauty methods for finding rational points on curves.

When I'm not thinking about math, I enjoy board games, listening to classical music, playing the violin, viola, and piano, and occasionally composing.
